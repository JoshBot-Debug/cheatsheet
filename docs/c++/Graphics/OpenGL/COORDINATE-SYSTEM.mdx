# Coordinate Systems & MVP

* Local space
* World space
* View space
* Clip space
* Screen space

## Local Space
Local space is also sometimes called **Model space** or **Object Space**
* Local space is the coordinate system relative to an object's own origin, usually at `(0,0,0)`.
* The cube (or model) vertices range from `(-1,-1,-1) to (1,1,1)`. It's origin is at `(0,0,0)`

### **Transformation**:
Local space to World space uses the **Model Matrix**.
$$
\mathbf{v}_{world} = M_{model} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{local}
$$

World space to Local space uses the **Inverse Model Matrix**.
$$
\mathbf{v}_{local}= M_{model}^{-1} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{world}
$$

**How to calculate $M_{model}^{-1}$**:
An model matrix is composed of Translation ($T$), Rotation ($R$), and Scale ($S$):
$$
M_{model} = T \cdot R \cdot S
$$
To invert it analytically without doing an expensive full matrix inversion calculation, you reverse the operations and invert each individual property:
$$
M_{model}^{-1} = S^{-1} \cdot R^{-1} \cdot T^{-1}
$$
* $T^{-1}$ (Inverse Translation): Negate the translation values (move backward instead of forward).
* $R^{-1}$ (Inverse Rotation): Transpose the rotation matrix (since pure rotation matrices are orthogonal, their transpose equals their inverse), or invert the quaternion.
* $S^{-1}$ (Inverse Scale): Reciprocate the scale factors (divide by the scale instead of multiplying, e.g., $1/scale_x$).

## World space
World space places all objects into a common coordinate system. All objects relative to world origin.

```text
Cube local vertex = (1,1,1)
Cube position in world = (5,0,0)
Cube world space = (6,1,1)
```

### **Transformation**:
World space to View space uses the **View Matrix**.
$$
\mathbf{v}_{view} = M_{view} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{world}
$$

View space to World space uses the **Inverse View Matrix**.
$$
\mathbf{v}_{world} = M_{view}^{-1} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{view}
$$

## View Space
In view space, coordinates are relative to the camera.

The camera is treated as being at: `(0,0,0)`
looking along a standard direction (often −Z in OpenGL-style conventions).

**World:**
```text
Camera = (0,0,10)
Object = (0,0,0)
```

**View space:**
```text
Camera = (0,0,0)
Object = (0,0,-10)
```
Everything is transformed so the camera becomes the origin.

### **Transformation**:
View space to Clip space uses the **Projection Matrix**.
$$
\mathbf{v}_{clip} = M_{proj} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{view}
$$

Clip space to View space uses the **Inverse Projection Matrix**.
$$
\mathbf{v}_{view} = M_{proj}^{-1} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{clip}
$$

## Clip Space
A specialized space where coordinates are evaluated to see if they are visible on screen. If a vertex lies outside the boundaries defined by its $w$ component (i.e., $-w \le x, y, z \le w$), it is "clipped" (discarded).

### **Transformation**:
Clip space to NDC (Normalized Device Coordinates) **Perspective Divide**.
$$
\mathbf{v}_{ndc} = \begin{bmatrix} x/w \\ y/w \\ z/w \end{bmatrix}
$$

NDC to Clip space **Perspective Multiply**.
$$
\mathbf{v}_{clip} = \begin{bmatrix} nx*w \\ ny*w \\ nz*w \end{bmatrix}
$$

You've done this or copied it a million times:
```glsl
vec4 clipPosition = projection * view * model * vec4(vPosition, 1.0);
vec3 ndc = clipPosition.xyz / clipPosition.w;
```

## Screen Space
The actual pixel coordinates of your monitor/window (e.g., from `0` to `1920` on the X-axis, and `0` to `1080` on the Y-axis).

## Forward pipeline

Step | Operation | Matrix / Equation | Resulting Space
|:---|:---|:---|:---
Start | Mesh Data | Initial Vertex | Local Space
1 | Apply Model Matrix | $\mathbf{v}_{w} = M_{model} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{l}$​ | World Space
2 | Apply View Matrix | $\mathbf{v}_{v} = M_{view} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{w}$​ | View Space
3 | Apply Projection Matrix | $\mathbf{v}_{c} = M_{proj} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{v}$​ | Clip Space
4 | Perspective Divide (GPU) | $\mathbf{v}_{ndc} = \mathbf{v}_{c}.xyz / \mathbf{v}_{c}.w$ | NDC Space
5 | Viewport Transform (GPU) | $\mathbf{v}_{s} = M_{viewport} \cdot \mathbf{v}_{ndc}$ | Screen Space

::::info
Basically the **backward pipeline** is the same but with the inverse matrix.
For NDC it's multiple by `w` instead of divide.
For Screen space it's divide by $M_{viewport} instead of multiply.
 ::::
